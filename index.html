The Evolution Of Computers

The First Generation

Pascaline

A mechanical calculator to add and subtract two numbers directly and to perform multiplication and division through repeated addition or subtraction.

Stepped reckoner

First digital mechanical calculator that could perform all four arithmetic operations.

Difference engine

An automatic mechanical calculator designed to tabulate polynomial functions.

The Second Generation

Atanasoff-Berry Computer

A machine that could solve 29 simultaneous equations with 29 unknowns.

Colossus

A machine that broke codes used by the German army in World War II

ENIAC

The first general purpose programmable electronic computer used extensively for calculations during the design of the hydrogen bomb.

EDVAC

One of the earliest binary electronic computers designed to be a stored-program computer.

The Third Generation

LARC

considered among the first supercomputers; used high-speed drum memory.

IBM 7030 Stretch

IBM's first transistorized supercomputer.

The Fourth Generation

CDC 6600

First successful supercomputer.
