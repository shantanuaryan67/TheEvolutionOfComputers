The Evolution of Computers

-2500

abacus

the first known calculator; laid the foundations for positional notation.

1642

Pascaline

mechanical calculator with a controlled carry mechanism.

1672

Stepped Reckoner

could multiply numbers of up to 12 digits to give a 16 digit result.

1804

jacquard loom

an automatic loom controlled by punched cards.

1822

difference engine

an automatic mechanical calculator designed to tabulate polynomial functions.

1833

Analytical Engine

mechanical general-purpose computer that could be described in modern terms as Turing-complete.

1884

Comptometer

first commercially successful key-driven mechanical calculator.

1930

differential analyser

A mechanical analogue computer designed to solve differential equations by integration, using wheel-and-disc mechanisms.

1938

Z1

first mechanical binary programmable computer.

1940

Z2

Used the same mechanical memory as Z1 but replaced the arithmetic and control logic with electrical relay circuits.

1941

Z3

World's first working programmable, fully automatic digital computer.

1942

Atanasoff–Berry computer

First automatic electronic digital computer designed only to solve systems of linear equations; first machine to calculate using vacuum tubes.

1943

Colossus

Used thermionic valves to perform Boolean and counting operations; regarded as the world's first programmable, electronic, digital computer.

1944

Harvard Mark I

General purpose electromechanical computer; became the second program-controlled machine.

1945

Z4

World's first commercial digital computer.

1946

ENIAC

First electronic general-purpose computer. It was Turing-complete, digital and able to solve "a large class of numerical problems" through reprogramming.

1949

EDSAC

First stored program executed on the EDSAC computer, which used paper tape input–output. also the first complete, fully functional von Neumann architecture computer.
